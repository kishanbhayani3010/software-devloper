One sunny afternoon, Nobita Nobi was lying on the floor, groaning as usual. “I wish I could escape homework forever!” he complained. Suddenly, Doraemon pulled out a strange gadget from his pocket. “It’s called the Time Seed!” Doraemon said. “Plant it, and it grows into a garden where time moves differently.” Nobita’s eyes lit up. “So I can finish homework instantly?” “Or… make things worse,” Doraemon warned. But Nobita didn’t listen. He planted the seed in the backyard. Within seconds, a glowing garden appeared, filled with sparkling plants and floating clocks. Excited, Nobita stepped inside. Time sped up—his homework finished in minutes! But when he stepped out… Everything had changed. His friends, Shizuka Minamoto, Takeshi Gouda, and Suneo Honekawa looked older! “Nobita! Where were you for three days?” Shizuka asked. Nobita panicked. “Three days?!” Meanwhile, the garden kept growing, pulling more of the world into its strange time flow. Trees aged instantly, flowers bloomed and withered in seconds. “This is bad!” Doraemon said. “The Time Seed is out of control!” To fix it, Nobita had to go back inside the garden and find its glowing core. But inside, time was wildly unstable—he aged, then became young again, over and over. Finally, remembering Doraemon’s advice, Nobita stayed calm and found the core. He pulled it out just in time. The garden vanished. Everything returned to normal. Nobita sighed in relief. “I guess shortcuts aren’t always the best idea…” Doraemon smiled. “Exactly. Some things take time for a reason.” Nobita looked at his homework… and for once, started doing it properly.
